how to deal with pressure and difficult customers while performing SRE tier2/3 support

This is a **high-stakes behavioral question** for Pico. They want to know:

- Can you stay calm during incidents?
- Can you handle **frustrated trading clients**?
- Can you communicate clearly while debugging complex systems?

You don’t need a perfect story—you need a **structured, confident approach**.

---

# ✅ Strong, interview-ready answer

Writing

In high-pressure situations, I focus on staying calm, structured, and communicative.

When dealing with incidents, my first priority is to quickly understand the issue and its impact, then begin a structured troubleshooting process while keeping stakeholders informed.

With difficult or frustrated customers, I focus on clear and transparent communication. I acknowledge their concerns, explain what we know so far in simple terms, and provide regular updates so they feel confident the issue is being actively addressed.

In my experience supporting real-time market data systems, I’ve learned that clients are often under pressure themselves, so maintaining professionalism and focusing on resolution helps de-escalate situations.

Overall, I try to balance technical troubleshooting with clear communication to ensure both the issue is resolved and the client remains informed and reassured.

# 🔥 If they push deeper (very likely)

## 💬 “What if the client is angry or blaming you?”

Strong answer:

> I focus on not taking it personally and understanding that they’re reacting to business impact.
> 
> I acknowledge their concern, keep communication factual, and focus on resolving the issue rather than assigning blame.
> 
> Keeping them updated regularly usually helps reduce frustration.

---

## 💬 “How do you prioritize during multiple incidents?”

> I prioritize based on business impact, such as system outages or data issues affecting trading.
> 
> I also communicate clearly with stakeholders so expectations are managed while I work through the highest priority issues.
