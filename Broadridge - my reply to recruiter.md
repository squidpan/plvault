**5. Tell me about your hands-on experience with cash management or money movement workflows — specifically wires, ACH, checks, transfers, or related payment flows. What part of the process did you own?**

My experience has been primarily focused on financial systems, business analysis, and operational workflows rather than directly executing payment operations such as wires or ACH processing.

Throughout my career, I have worked closely with business users, operations teams, and technology teams supporting financial services platforms where the accuracy, timeliness, and distribution of financial data were critical to downstream business processes. My responsibilities included requirements gathering, process analysis, stakeholder coordination, testing/UAT, production support, and helping ensure that business workflows were properly represented in system functionality.

Although my hands-on experience is stronger on the business analysis and financial systems side than on payment operations themselves, I have consistently ramped up quickly in complex financial services environments and am actively expanding my knowledge of ACH, wire transfers, cash management, and money movement workflows.

**6. Describe a project where you gathered business requirements from users and translated them for development. How did you handle conflicting requirements, testing/UAT, and delivery through the SDLC?**

One example was a modernization initiative involving a real-time streaming fixed-income market data distribution platform at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York.

Existing consumers received pricing data through Oracle views. As part of an AWS modernization effort, the business requirement was that those same consumers be able to retrieve equivalent data through REST APIs without losing required fields, business rules, or functionality.

I worked with business users, consuming application teams, developers, and operations support teams to map Oracle view fields to JSON payload fields, document business rules, and define requirements, user stories, and acceptance criteria around data parity.

Testing involved comparing Oracle view extracts against REST API payloads to confirm field-level accuracy and business-rule consistency. The primary conflicts were not disagreements about business functionality but competing testing schedules, environment availability, and different consumer cutover timelines. I worked with stakeholders to coordinate testing windows, support defect triage, and obtain UAT signoff before production migration.

This effort followed the full SDLC process from requirements gathering through development support, testing, UAT, release readiness, and production rollout.

**7. This role requires ramping quickly in a financial services environment with client interaction. Can you give an example of a client-facing or stakeholder-heavy initiative in banking/brokerage where your domain knowledge helped you solve a business problem fast?**

During a multi-year cloud modernization initiative, I worked with numerous consumer application teams that depended on fixed-income market data for trading, analytical, and operational functions.

Over time, I developed a strong understanding of fixed-income pricing, market data distribution, consumer application dependencies, and the operational impact of data quality and availability issues. Because of that domain knowledge, I was able to engage quickly with business stakeholders, development teams, operations staff, and consumer application teams without requiring extensive ramp-up time.

As new AWS-based interfaces were introduced, stakeholders needed confidence that critical market data would remain accurate, complete, and available after migration. My understanding of both the business context and technical architecture allowed me to identify dependencies quickly, facilitate discussions between stakeholders, support testing and rollout activities, and help resolve issues before they affected downstream consumers.

This combination of financial services domain knowledge, business analysis experience, and stakeholder management enabled me to contribute effectively and help accelerate delivery in a highly regulated financial environment.
