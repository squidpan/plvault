## Swimming and Osteoporosis: Key Insights

While swimming is a safe, low-impact exercise that offers significant benefits for joint health and cardiovascular fitness, **it is not effective for increasing bone mineral density (BMD) or treating osteoporosis**.  Unlike weight-bearing and resistance exercises, swimming does not apply mechanical stress to bones, which is essential for stimulating bone growth.

**Weight-Bearing Exercises**  
These help maintain or improve bone density by working against gravity. Recommended options include:

- **Brisk walking**: Aim for 30 minutes most days of the week at a moderate intensity (RPE 11–14 or 40–60% of heart rate reserve).
- **Stair climbing**: A low-impact, effective way to strengthen legs and hips.
- **Tai Chi**: Improves balance, coordination, and bone density in the spine and hips.
- **Dancing** (e.g., waltz, salsa, or gentle ballet): Combines weight-bearing, strength, and balance benefits. 
- **Hiking**: Provides variable impact, especially on uphill/downhill terrain.
- **Low-impact aerobics or elliptical training**: Safe for those with joint concerns.
    

**Resistance (Strength) Exercises**  
These build muscle and stimulate bone growth, especially in targeted areas. Focus on **2–3 days per week**, using proper form:

- **Wall push-ups**: Stand facing a wall, hands at shoulder height, and push your body away. Do 2 sets of 10–15 reps.
- **Bodyweight squats**: Perform slowly with support (e.g., chair or wall) to maintain balance.
- **Resistance band exercises**: Use bands for rows, bicep curls, and shoulder presses—safe and effective.
- **Leg lifts (lying down)**: Lie on your back, raise one leg at a time, and hold for 8 seconds. Avoid arching your lower back.
- **Heel raises**: Stand with support, rise onto your toes, then slowly lower. Builds calf and lower leg strength. 

**Key Tips**

- **Start slowly** and consult a physical therapist or certified trainer experienced in osteoporosis.
- Avoid high-impact jumps, deep backbends, or twisting motions that increase fracture risk.
- Combine resistance and balance training to reduce fall risk—**the most effective strategy for fracture prevention**. 
- Aim for **30 minutes of weight-bearing activity daily** and **2–3 resistance sessions per week** for best results.
