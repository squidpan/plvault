## East of eden

In the Bible, =="East of Eden" is the **physical location where Cain is banished** after murdering Abel [Genesis 4](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Genesis%204&version=NIV)==. 

The Biblical Context

- **The Land of Nod**: Genesis 4:16 states Cain lived in Nod, **east of Eden**.
- **The Symbolism**: "Nod" means **wandering**. It symbolizes a life separated from God's presence.
- **The Contrast**: Eden was paradise. East of Eden represents a **harsh, fallen world**.

The Cultural Impact

- **John Steinbeck's Novel**: The famous 1952 book _East of Eden_ **reinterprets the biblical story**.
- **The Trask Family**: Characters Adam, Caleb, and Aron mirror Adam, **Cain, and Abel**.
- **The Core Theme**: The book explores free will via the Hebrew word **timshel** (thou mayest rule over sin) [Genesis 4:7]
