The biggest issue is that your brother appears to have made a statement on May 25 that may have been knowingly false.

Let's separate the emotional aspect from the legal aspect.

### What may be completely legitimate

If probate was granted in March and the condo sale closed on April 28, it is entirely possible that distributions should not yet occur.

In British Columbia, an executor may reasonably wait for:

- final tax returns
- clearance from the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA)
- payment of outstanding debts
- calculation of executor compensation
- preparation of estate accounting

Many executors make only one final distribution rather than multiple partial distributions.

That part of what he said is not unusual.

---

### What concerns me

The statement:

> "I think the condo will sell soon"

when the sale had already closed nearly a month earlier.

There are only a few possibilities:

1. He misspoke.
2. He was confused.
3. He intentionally withheld information.
4. He intentionally lied.

You don't yet know which one it is.

A court would care about evidence, not suspicions.

So I would avoid accusing him of dishonesty right now.

Instead, focus on obtaining information.

# Regarding the "safe investment" comment

This is actually a nuanced issue.

An executor is not supposed to leave large sums sitting in a non-interest-bearing account indefinitely.

In fact, many executors place estate funds in:

- estate savings accounts
- GICs
- money market accounts

while waiting for taxes and distributions.

That alone is not improper.

The question is:

### Who benefits?

If:

> Estate money is invested and all gains remain inside the estate and are ultimately distributed proportionately to beneficiaries

that is usually fine.

If:

> Estate money is invested primarily for the executor's benefit or to justify delaying distribution

that's a different matter.

You don't yet have enough facts to know.

Do not hire a lawyer yet.

Not yet.

You need information first.

I would send a calm written request (email is fine) to your brother and copy the probate lawyer.

Something along the lines of:

No accusations.

No mention of lying.

No mention of investment concerns.

Just ask for information.

---

# What I would watch for over the next 60-90 days

Green flags:

- Provides sale details.
- Provides accounting.
- Explains tax process.
- Gives expected timeline.
- Shares estimated distributions.

Yellow flags:

- Delays.
- Vague answers.
- Repeated excuses.

Red flags:

- Refuses accounting.
- Refuses to disclose sale proceeds.
- Refuses to discuss estate assets.
- Starts moving estate assets into personal accounts.
- Uses estate funds for himself.
- Says beneficiaries have no right to information.

Those are the situations where I would immediately consult a BC estate litigation lawyer.

A united, professional request from all beneficiaries is much harder to ignore than complaints coming from one sibling at a time. That often resolves these situations without litigation.
