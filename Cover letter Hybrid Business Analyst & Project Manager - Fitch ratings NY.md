# Hello

Dear Fitch Ratings Hiring Team,

I've spent nearly a decade translating complex business requirements into actionable technical deliverables—most recently at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York, where I bridged the gap between business stakeholders and development teams to migrate streaming financial market data services operating on-premises to the AWS cloud environment. When I saw Fitch's opening for a Hybrid Business Analyst & Project Manager, I recognized an opportunity to apply this exact skill set to help BRM teams focus on what they do best: driving revenue and client relationships.

At the Fed, I managed the complete requirements lifecycle in an Agile environment, breaking down high-level epics into developer-ready user stories in Jira while coordinating releases across Dev, QA, and Production environments. I defined REST API endpoints using Swagger, documented technical implementations in Confluence, and served as the primary liaison between business units and technology teams—reducing communication overhead and accelerating delivery timelines. This mirrors precisely what you need: someone who can capture business needs from BRM verticals and deliver clear specifications that development teams can execute against.

My experience spans the full SDLC across financial services environments at AIG, Fidelity, and JP Morgan Chase. I'm proficient with your core toolset—Jira, Confluence, and Agile methodologies—and I understand the pressure points on both sides: business teams need solutions fast, while technical teams need requirements that are complete and feasible.

What excites me about Fitch is your commitment to reducing administrative burden so revenue-generating teams can focus on clients. I've done exactly this: streamlining processes, managing concurrent projects, and ensuring stakeholder alignment without direct authority. I'd welcome the chance to discuss how my Federal Reserve experience positions me to deliver immediate value to your Revenue Enablement team.

Sincerely,

Paul Lyu
