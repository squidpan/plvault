Dear ,

We hope you are doing well.

First, we would like to acknowledge and thank you for the time and effort involved in administering Mom and Dad's estate. We understand that acting as executor carries significant responsibilities and that there are legal, tax, and administrative matters that must be completed before the estate can be finalized.

As beneficiaries of the estate, we would appreciate a status update regarding the administration process and the anticipated timeline for completion.

We understand that probate was granted earlier this year and that the downtown Vancouver condominium sale completed on or about April 28, 2026. Since we have not received any recent updates, we would appreciate information regarding the current status of the estate, including:

- Confirmation of the current estate assets and liabilities.
    
- Status of the downtown condominium sale, including the net proceeds received by the estate.
    
- Status of the second property and any plans regarding its disposition.
    
- Status of any tax filings, including whether there are outstanding tax obligations or a CRA clearance process underway.
    
- Whether any interim or final distributions are anticipated and, if so, the expected timeline.
    
- A general accounting of estate receipts, expenses, and balances to date, if available.
    

We fully understand that estate administration can take time and that certain funds may need to remain in the estate pending taxes, expenses, and final accounting. Our intention is simply to stay informed regarding the administration process and expected next steps.

For convenience and transparency, we are copying all beneficiaries and estate counsel on this message.

We would appreciate any update you can provide when time permits.

Thank you again for your efforts on behalf of the estate.

Sincerely,

[Claire, Jinie, paul]


```
A few strategic observations:

- **Copying the lawyer is smart.**
- The phrase **"For convenience and transparency, we are copying all beneficiaries and estate counsel"** signals that communication is now moving into a documented channel.
- The phrase **"net proceeds received by the estate"** is important. You're not just asking whether it sold; you're asking what the estate actually received after commissions and closing costs.
- The phrase **"general accounting"** is less confrontational than "formal accounting." It gives them room to cooperate voluntarily.

If they respond constructively, great.

If they ignore the request, refuse to provide information, or provide vague answers, the next step would likely be a consultation with a British Columbia estate lawyer about a formal demand for an executor's accounting. At that point, the fact that you first made a reasonable written request will help your position considerably.
```
